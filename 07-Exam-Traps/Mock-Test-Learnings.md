# Mock Test Learnings

Mock tests are not designed to predict your final score.

They are designed to identify gaps.

The goal is not to score 100%.

The goal is to discover what you do not know.

---

# How To Use Mock Tests Properly

For every incorrect question:

Do not simply memorize the answer.

Instead learn:

1. What concept is being tested?
2. Why is the answer correct?
3. Why are the other options wrong?
4. What service is associated with each option?

This transforms one question into multiple learning opportunities.

---

# Example Learning Pattern

Question:

Which AWS service provides threat detection?

Options:

A. Inspector

B. GuardDuty

C. CloudTrail

D. CloudWatch

Correct Answer:

GuardDuty

---

What To Learn

GuardDuty

- Threat Detection
- Suspicious Activity
- Compromised Credentials

Inspector

- Vulnerability Scanning

CloudTrail

- Audit Logs

CloudWatch

- Monitoring

One question teaches four services.

---

# Mock Test Analysis Process

After every mock:

Step 1

Review all incorrect questions.

---

Step 2

Group mistakes by topic.

Example:

- IAM
- Security
- Storage
- Pricing

---

Step 3

Revise only weak areas.

---

Step 4

Retake another mock.

---

Step 5

Repeat until mistakes reduce.

---

# Common Mock Test Mistakes

- Memorizing answers
- Ignoring explanations
- Retaking the same mock immediately
- Not reviewing mistakes
- Focusing only on score

---

# Important Reminder

Low mock scores are not failures.

They are feedback.

A 50% mock score that reveals weaknesses is more useful than a 90% score obtained through memorization.
