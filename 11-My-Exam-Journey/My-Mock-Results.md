# My Mock Results

## Introduction

Mock tests played an important role in my AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02) preparation.

More important than the scores themselves was understanding:

* Why I got questions wrong
* Which domains needed more attention
* Which AWS services I was confusing

This document summarizes my mock test experience and key learnings.

---

## Mock Test 1

### Score

59%

### What It Showed

This was my baseline assessment.

The biggest weaknesses were:

* IAM
* Security Concepts
* Pricing
* VPC Fundamentals

### Key Lesson

I understood that simply recognizing service names was not enough.

I needed to understand service use cases.

---

## Mock Test 2

### Score

62%

### What It Showed

There was improvement compared to the first mock.

However, I was still making mistakes in:

* Networking
* Security Services
* Service Comparisons

### Key Lesson

Understanding why an answer is correct is more important than memorizing answers.

---

## Mock Test 3

### Score

51%

### What It Showed

This mock was noticeably harder.

It exposed deeper conceptual gaps that previous mocks did not reveal.

Areas requiring improvement:

* AWS Service Selection
* Scenario-Based Questions
* Pricing Models

### Key Lesson

A lower score does not always mean less knowledge.

Sometimes a harder mock simply reveals gaps that need attention.

---

## Mock Test 4

### Score

69.02%

### What It Showed

This was the point where my understanding became much more stable.

I was able to:

* Identify AWS services faster
* Eliminate incorrect answers
* Understand common AWS keywords

### Key Lesson

Confidence comes from understanding concepts, not from memorizing dumps.

---

## Official AWS Practice Questions

Before the actual exam, the most useful practice resource for me was the Official AWS Cloud Practitioner Practice Questions available through AWS Skill Builder.

I carefully reviewed:

* Incorrect answers
* AWS wording patterns
* Service comparisons
* Security concepts

This helped me understand the actual style of AWS questions much better.

---

## What I Learned From Mock Tests

### Lesson 1

Security services are heavily tested.

Especially:

* IAM
* GuardDuty
* Inspector
* CloudTrail
* CloudWatch

---

### Lesson 2

Service comparison questions appear frequently.

Examples:

* SNS vs SQS
* WAF vs Shield
* EBS vs EFS vs S3
* CloudTrail vs CloudWatch

---

### Lesson 3

Elimination is one of the most powerful exam strategies.

Many questions can be solved by removing obviously incorrect options.

---

### Lesson 4

Mock scores are not for ego.

They are for discovering weaknesses before the actual exam.

---

## Final Takeaway

My mock scores were never perfect.

In fact, some of them were quite average.

What mattered was learning from every mistake and continuously improving.

The biggest improvement came from:

* Understanding concepts
* Reviewing incorrect answers
* Learning service use cases
* Practicing elimination

Those lessons helped me achieve a passing score of **761** in the AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02) exam.
