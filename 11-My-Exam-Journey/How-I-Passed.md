# How I Passed AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02)

## My Honest Preparation Story

If I am being completely honest, I did not spend weeks or months preparing for this certification.

I started my serious preparation roughly 24 hours before the exam.

At that point, I had very limited knowledge of the AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02) exam pattern, scoring process, and question style.

In fact, one of the funniest things is that I learned about scored and unscored questions only around 20 minutes before entering the exam.

---

## The Beginning

I started preparing in the afternoon at around 3:00 PM on the day before the exam.

Instead of rushing directly into dumps and memorizing answers, I decided to understand the concepts first.

My goal was simple:

If a question appeared in a different format, I still wanted to understand why the answer was correct.

So I focused on learning the concepts behind the services rather than memorizing questions.

---

## The Long Night

I continued studying throughout the evening and night.

Most of my focus was on:

* Cloud Concepts
* Security Services
* Core AWS Services
* Pricing Models
* Service Comparisons

I stayed awake until around 5:00 AM.

After that, I took some rest.

---

## Exam Day

The next day, I continued revising whenever I got time.

I revised notes during classes and used every available break to go through important concepts.

As the exam time approached, I took permission and headed to the exam center.

Even during the final minutes before the exam, I was still revising.

Most of my revision came from the handbook and final revision notes that I had prepared.

---

## The Most Useful Resource

The single most useful resource before the exam was the Official AWS Practice Questions available through AWS Skill Builder.

This helped me understand:

* Question patterns
* AWS wording
* Elimination techniques
* Scenario-based questions

More importantly, I carefully reviewed every mistake I made.

I did not focus only on correct answers.

I focused on understanding why I got certain questions wrong.

That made a huge difference.

---

## What Actually Helped Me

The biggest lesson I learned is:

Understanding beats memorization.

Many learners spend hours memorizing dumps.

Personally, I found that understanding the services and their use cases was much more valuable.

When you understand:

* What a service does
* When to use it
* Why it exists

many questions become surprisingly easy.

---

## Elimination Is The Key

If there is one exam strategy I would recommend, it is elimination.

Most questions can be reduced to two options very quickly.

Once you understand AWS services and their use cases, eliminating incorrect answers becomes much easier.

Many times, you may not immediately know the correct answer.

But you can identify which answers are definitely wrong.

That alone improves your chances significantly.

---

## My Advice About Dumps

Practice questions can be useful.

However, blindly memorizing dumps is not a strategy I would recommend.

AWS often tests concepts, use cases, and service selection.

A memorized answer may fail when the scenario changes.

Understanding the concepts gives you much more confidence during the actual exam.

---

## Final Thoughts

I walked into the exam with far less preparation time than most people would recommend.

I relied heavily on:

* Understanding concepts
* Service comparisons
* Security fundamentals
* Official AWS practice questions
* Last-minute revision notes

The experience taught me that confidence, understanding, and smart revision are often more important than simply spending the highest number of hours studying.

If you are preparing for AWS Cloud Practitioner, focus on understanding first and memorizing second.

Good luck, and I hope this repository helps you on your certification journey.
