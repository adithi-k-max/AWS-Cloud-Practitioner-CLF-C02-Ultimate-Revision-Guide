# Mistakes I Made While Preparing for AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02)

## Introduction

No preparation journey is perfect.

While preparing for the AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02) exam, I made several mistakes that slowed me down, created unnecessary stress, or reduced my efficiency.

This document is a collection of those mistakes so that future learners can avoid them.

---

## Mistake 1 — Starting Too Late

The biggest mistake I made was delaying my preparation.

I started serious preparation only about 24 hours before the exam.

While it worked out for me, I would not recommend this approach.

Having more time would have allowed me to:

* Practice more questions
* Revise more comfortably
* Reduce exam stress
* Sleep properly before the exam

---

## Mistake 2 — Underestimating Security Topics

Initially, I thought security would be a small part of the exam.

I was wrong.

Services such as:

* IAM
* GuardDuty
* Inspector
* CloudTrail
* CloudWatch
* WAF
* Shield

appear frequently throughout the exam.

Security is one of the most important domains in CLF-C02.

---

## Mistake 3 — Not Understanding The Exam Pattern Early

I learned some exam details very late in my preparation.

For example:

* Number of questions
* Scored vs unscored questions
* Question style
* AWS wording patterns

Understanding the exam pattern earlier would have improved my confidence.

---

## Mistake 4 — Spending Too Much Time Looking For Resources

At the beginning, I spent time jumping between:

* PDFs
* Videos
* Notes
* Question Banks
* Websites

Instead of constantly searching for better resources, it is usually better to pick a few good resources and complete them properly.

---

## Mistake 5 — Not Focusing On Service Comparisons Earlier

Many AWS questions are actually comparison questions.

Examples:

* CloudTrail vs CloudWatch
* SNS vs SQS
* GuardDuty vs Inspector
* WAF vs Shield
* EBS vs EFS vs S3

I should have focused on these earlier.

Understanding comparisons makes question elimination much easier.

---

## Mistake 6 — Thinking More Questions Equals More Learning

At one point, I believed solving more questions automatically meant better preparation.

That is not always true.

The real learning happens when you understand:

* Why the answer is correct
* Why the other options are wrong

Quality of review is more important than quantity of questions.

---

## Mistake 7 — Not Reviewing Wrong Answers Properly

One of the biggest opportunities for learning comes from mistakes.

Initially, I focused too much on checking the correct answer.

Later, I realized that reviewing wrong answers teaches much more.

Every wrong answer reveals a knowledge gap.

---

## Mistake 8 — Ignoring AWS Keywords

AWS questions often contain keywords that directly reveal the answer.

Examples:

* Audit → CloudTrail
* Monitoring → CloudWatch
* Threat Detection → GuardDuty
* Vulnerability Assessment → Inspector
* DNS → Route 53
* CDN → CloudFront

Learning these mappings earlier would have saved time.

---

## Mistake 9 — Thinking Dumps Alone Are Enough

Dumps may help expose you to question styles.

However, relying entirely on memorization is risky.

AWS frequently tests concepts and use cases.

Understanding the service is far more useful than memorizing an answer.

---

## Mistake 10 — Not Trusting Elimination Enough

During preparation, I sometimes felt I needed to know every answer immediately.

In reality, elimination is one of the strongest exam strategies.

Many questions can be solved by removing obviously incorrect options.

Understanding services well makes elimination much easier.

---

## What I Would Do Differently

If I had to prepare again, I would:

1. Start earlier.
2. Focus on service comparisons first.
3. Learn security concepts thoroughly.
4. Use official AWS resources more extensively.
5. Analyze every incorrect practice question.
6. Spend less time searching for resources.
7. Sleep properly before the exam.

---

## Final Lesson

The AWS Cloud Practitioner exam is not a memory test.

It is a test of understanding.

The more you understand:

* What a service does
* When it should be used
* Why it exists

the easier the exam becomes.

If you learn from your mistakes during preparation, the actual exam feels much less intimidating.
